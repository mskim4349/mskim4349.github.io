A neural-network framework that maps a single instrumented spherical-indentation
load–depth response to the underlying elasto-plastic parameters (yield strength,
hardening exponent, hardening modulus). The training data is generated by a
parametric FE sweep; the network learns the inverse mapping that classical
Oliver–Pharr corrections only approximate, and works on hardening behavior far
from the calibration points used by analytical methods.

**Why it matters.** Indentation is one of the few mechanical tests that scales
to microscale samples — thin films, single grains, additively manufactured
pillars — where extracting bulk constitutive parameters is otherwise hard. A
reliable single-curve parameter identification unlocks high-throughput
characterization of these geometries.

**Reference.** *Determination of Material Parameters from Single Spherical
Indentation Data Using Artificial Neural Networks*, **Journal of Applied
Mechanics** 93(3) 031008, 2026 — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1115/1.4070645).
