2025

  1. CMAME
    A Multiscale Graph Neural Network Surrogate for History-Dependent Elasto-Plastic Homogenization
    Myungsung Kim, A. Coauthor, and B. Coauthor
    Computer Methods in Applied Mechanics and Engineering, 2025
    Abs Bib

    We propose a graph neural network surrogate for the offline–online multiscale modeling of elasto-plastic composites. The model encodes the microstructural mesh as a graph and predicts the homogenized stress response under arbitrary strain histories, achieving 100×–1000× speed-ups over direct FE² solves while preserving frame indifference and material objectivity.

2024

  1. IJP
    Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Inverse Identification of Elasto-Plastic Parameters from Instrumented Indentation
    Myungsung Kim and C. Coauthor
    International Journal of Plasticity, 2024
    Abs Bib

    A PINN-based inverse framework recovers yield stress, hardening modulus, and the Hollomon exponent from a single load–depth curve without requiring an analytical Oliver–Pharr correction.

  2. JMPS
    Thermodynamically Consistent Neural-Network Constitutive Models for Anisotropic Plasticity
    Myungsung Kim, D. Coauthor, and E. Coauthor
    Journal of the Mechanics and Physics of Solids, 2024
    Abs Bib

    We embed a free-energy and dissipation potential parameterization inside a feed-forward network so that the resulting model satisfies the Clausius–Duhem inequality by construction, while remaining expressive enough to capture anisotropic hardening observed in rolled aluminum sheets.

2023

  1. CompMatSci
    A Convolutional Surrogate for Phase-Field Damage Evolution in Heterogeneous Microstructures
    Myungsung Kim and F. Coauthor
    Computational Materials Science, 2023
    Abs Bib

    A U-Net surrogate predicts the next-step phase-field variable from the current strain energy density field, enabling rapid screening of microstructural designs for damage tolerance.

  2. IJSS
    Data-Driven Reduced-Order Modeling of Residual Stress in Multi-Pass Welding
    Myungsung Kim and G. Coauthor
    International Journal of Solids and Structures, 2023
    Abs Bib

    We combine proper orthogonal decomposition with a recurrent neural network to reproduce full residual stress fields from multi-pass welding simulations at a fraction of the computational cost.
